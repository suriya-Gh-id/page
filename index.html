Mother Teresa

Mother Teresa was a beacon of compassion, dedicating her life to serving the poorest and most vulnerable. Her selfless work through the Missionaries of Charity continues to inspire kindness and humanitarian service around the world.

Compassion in action: Mother Teresa's selfless service to humanity.

Timeline of Mother Teresa's Life

  • 1910 – Birth: Born on August 26, 1910, in Skopje, Macedonia, as Anjezë Gonxhe Bojaxhiu.
  • 1928 – Call to Religious Life: At age 18, Anjezë left her home to join the Sisters of Loreto in Ireland, taking the name Sister Mary Teresa.
  • 1929 – Arrival in India: Sister Teresa arrived in Calcutta (now Kolkata) to begin her novitiate and teaching at a convent school.
  • 1931 – First Religious Vows: Took her first vows, becoming a nun, and adopted the name "Teresa" after Saint Thérèse of Lisieux.
  • 1946 – 'Call Within a Call': During a train journey, she experienced a divine call to leave the convent and serve the poor directly.
  • 1948 – Founding of the Missionaries of Charity: With permission from the Vatican, Mother Teresa left the Loreto order to start her work in the slums of Calcutta.
  • 1950 – Official Recognition of Missionaries of Charity: The Missionaries of Charity was officially established as a religious congregation by Pope Pius XII.
  • 1952 – Establishment of Nirmal Hriday: Opened her first home for the dying in Calcutta to care for those in their final stages of life.
  • 1953 – Shishu Bhavan Orphanage: Opened her first orphanage, offering shelter and care to abandoned children.
  • 1962 – Padma Shri Award: Received one of India’s highest civilian honors for her charitable work.
  • 1965 – Expansion Beyond India: Pope Paul VI granted her permission to expand her order internationally.
  • 1971 – Pope John XXIII Peace Prize: Awarded for her efforts to promote peace and the care of the oppressed.
  • 1979 – Nobel Peace Prize: Honored for her tireless work to aid the suffering, she accepted the prize on behalf of the "poorest of the poor."
  • 1982 – Beirut, Lebanon: Crossed active war zones to rescue 37 children trapped in a hospital during the Lebanese Civil War.
  • 1985 – Address to the United Nations: Gave a moving speech at the 40th anniversary of the UN, emphasizing the importance of love and compassion.
  • 1991 – Return to Albania: Made her first visit to Albania, her homeland, after the fall of its Communist regime.
  • 1996 – Honorary U.S. Citizenship: Awarded honorary U.S. citizenship, one of only a few to receive this distinction.
  • 1997 – Final Days and Death: Stepped down as head of the Missionaries of Charity due to declining health and passed away on September 5, 1997.
  • 2003 – Beatification: Pope John Paul II beatified her, giving her the title "Blessed," a major step toward sainthood.
  • 2016 – Canonization: Declared a saint by Pope Francis on September 4, 2016, recognizing her as Saint Teresa of Calcutta.
